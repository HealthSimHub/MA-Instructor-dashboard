DAY-BY-DAY FACILITATION
DAY 1 · MON
Admin Orientation — What Does This Job Actually Look Like?
Revenue Cycle · EHR vs EMR · Insurance Overview · Setting the Week's Context
☀️ Huddle: 30 min 🏥 Shift: 80 min 🌙 Debrief: 30 min
☀️ MORNING HUDDLE ~30 min
  • 1
    Open with the cover slide and agenda card. SLIDE 1 SLIDE 2 — Walk through what the full week looks like. Let students know this week has 5 days, 9 module quizzes, and ends with a badge. Lay out the roadmap so nothing feels like a surprise.
  • 2
    Core concepts — EHR vs EMR and the revenue cycle. SLIDE 3 — Don't rush this. The EHR/EMR distinction trips people up on the exam. Use the vocab box as a read-along. Connect to what students already know: "You've been using our EMR all semester — which type is it?"
  • 3
    Real world anchor. Read the green callout box on Slide 3 aloud or ask a student to. The goal is for them to feel the stakes: every scheduling or coding error is money the clinic doesn't get paid. Their accuracy starts at the front desk.
  • 4
    Distribute the rubric and activities packet. Give students 2 minutes to scan both. Tell them this packet is their work for the week — they'll turn it in on Friday.
💡
FACILITATION TIP The revenue cycle is the connective tissue of the entire week. Every activity, every EMR task, and every exam question ties back to it. Spend a few extra minutes here — students who understand the cycle holistically perform better on all 9 module quizzes.
🏥 CLINIC SHIFT — Foundational Activities ~80 min
  • 1
    Activity 1 — Revenue Cycle Sort. ACT 1 — Individual, 10 minutes. Students put the 8 scrambled steps in order and write who is responsible. Don't help — let them struggle productively. This surfaces gaps you'll address in the debrief.
  • 2
    Pair-share and self-correct. After individual work, have students compare with a partner. Then reveal the answer key together as a class. The goal isn't grading — it's building the mental model. Students should write corrections in their own words.
  • 3
    Activity 2 — Insurance Matching. ACT 2 — Individual, 8 minutes. Then review together. Use this as a launch point for a quick discussion: Which insurance do most of your patients in this area have? What does that mean for how you check them in?
  • 4
    Introduce the week's patients. SLIDE 5 — Pull up the patient preview and go through all three cards. This is not a deep dive — it's a preview so students have context before they open the EMR on Day 2. Have them write the three names and chief complaints somewhere in their notes.
  • 5
    Free reading / guided notes. Remaining time — students work through their guided notes using their source material and notes from today's huddle. Circulate and check for misconceptions, especially around EHR vs EMR and the revenue cycle order.
⚠️
COMMON STUDENT CONFUSION — Day 1 Students frequently mix up EHR and EMR. The simplest fix: "EMR = one office, one visit. EHR = whole life, across systems." They also often put Coding before Check-In in the revenue cycle. Correct this firmly on Day 1 so it doesn't carry through the week.
🌙 END OF SHIFT — Debrief ~30 min
  • 1
    Cold call revenue cycle order — no notes. Go around the room and have each student name one step in order. If they get it wrong, the class helps. The goal is for every student to leave today knowing the 8 steps.
  • 2
    Exit ticket: "Name one thing that surprised you today about the administrative side of being an MA." Quick shares — this tells you where their mental model was before today and sets up Day 2 nicely.
  • 3
    Preview Day 2. "Tomorrow we open the EMR and schedule our first patients. Make sure your guided notes are started and your Activities packet is with you." Remind them the module quizzes begin Day 2.
DAY 2 · TUE
Scheduling + Beginning the Visit
Phone Screening · Appointment Types · Patient Check-In · EMR Live Work · Module 8 Quiz
☀️ Huddle: 25 min 🏥 Shift: 95 min 🌙 Debrief: 25 min
☀️ MORNING HUDDLE ~25 min
  • 1
    Quick revenue cycle check — popcorn style. No notes, no slide. One student starts, next picks up. Goal: every student can contribute at least one step. This is a 3-minute warmup, not a quiz.
  • 2
    Module roadmap slide. SLIDE 4 — Briefly orient students to all 9 modules. Tell them Module 8 (Scheduling) quiz is due at the end of class today. Walk through the 6 skills in Mod 8 quickly so they know what to expect.
  • 3
    Model a screening call. Before students do Activity 4, model one yourself. Use Robert Nguyen's scenario. Show what professional language sounds like, how to triage urgency, and what happens when a caller says symptoms are getting worse. Keep it to 3–4 minutes — just enough to anchor the activity.
🎯
CCMA EXAM CONNECTION — Module 8 The exam tests screening call protocol heavily — especially emergency call handling. Students need to know: recognize red flags, do NOT put patient on hold during a possible emergency, follow clinic protocol, and know when to direct patient to call 911. Make this explicit before they do the role play.
🏥 CLINIC SHIFT — Scheduling + Check-In Simulation ~95 min
  • 1
    Activity 4 — Phone Screening Role Play. ACT 4 — Partners, 15 minutes. One student plays the MA, one plays the caller. Then switch. Circulate and listen for: professional greeting, all 5 screening questions, correct triage decision, and the "plot twist" moment (worsening symptoms). Give specific verbal feedback in the moment.
  • 2
    EMR — Schedule all 3 patients. EMR MOD 8 — Marcus: established patient follow-up. Destiny: new patient sports physical. Robert: urgent same-day. Students must select the correct scheduling method and visit type for each. This is where the theory from Day 1 becomes real — spot-check that they're not just clicking through.
  • 3
    Activity 5 — Check-In Simulation Checklist. ACT 5 EMR — Students work through the 8-step check-in for Marcus Webb simultaneously in the EMR and on their paper checklist. This is the most important skill activity of the week. Walk the room constantly.
  • 4
    Copay collection. Make sure every student posts Marcus's $20 copay in the EMR correctly before moving on. This is a check-off item on the rubric. Students who skip this step will fail that row — flag it in real time.
  • 5
    Module 8 Quiz. MOD 8 — If class time allows, open the quiz in the last 20 minutes of the shift. Otherwise assign for homework tonight. Students who did the scheduling tasks in the EMR will find this quiz much easier — reinforce that connection.
🔵
DIFFERENTIATION NOTE Destiny's check-in is more complex than Marcus's because of the minor patient requirement. If students finish Marcus early, have them attempt Destiny's check-in as a challenge. If students are struggling with Marcus, stay focused there — don't rush to Destiny. The minor patient rules will be covered again on Day 3.
🌙 END OF SHIFT — Debrief ~25 min
  • 1
    Class discussion — what did the role play teach you? "What was the hardest part of the screening call?" Students almost always say the plot twist (worsening symptoms). Use this to reinforce protocol: calm, don't hold, escalate.
  • 2
    Verify EMR check-ins are complete. Walk the room and spot-check that all 3 patients are scheduled and Marcus is checked in with copay posted. This is a soft accountability moment before Day 3's deeper work.
  • 3
    Preview Day 3. "Tomorrow we code the visit — ICD, CPT, HCPCS — and we dig into the referral for Marcus. If you haven't finished Activity 2 or 5, complete those tonight."
DAY 3 · WED
Coding + Revenue Cycle Deep Dive
ICD-10-CM · CPT · HCPCS · Medical Necessity · Referrals · Prior Auth · Module 9 Quiz
☀️ Huddle: 25 min 🏥 Shift: 95 min 🌙 Debrief: 25 min
☀️ MORNING HUDDLE ~25 min
  • 1
    Revenue cycle slide — follow the money. SLIDE 7 — Walk through all 8 steps with the visual. Today focuses on steps 3–5: eligibility, documentation, coding. Point out that the MA touches almost every step — this is why admin knowledge is on the CCMA exam.
  • 2
    Code types — quick distinction. Write on the board: ICD-10-CM = WHY (diagnosis). CPT = WHAT WAS DONE (procedure). HCPCS = STUFF (equipment, meds, supplies not in CPT). Give one real example of each from Marcus's visit. Students should write this down — it's almost certainly on their quiz.
  • 3
    Medical necessity check-in. Ask: "Why does it matter that the diagnosis code matches the procedure code?" Let 2–3 students respond before you explain. Build toward: if medical necessity isn't supported, the claim gets denied. That starts with the MA selecting the right code.
🎯
CCMA EXAM CONNECTION — High Frequency Topics Today ICD-10-CM vs CPT vs HCPCS is a guaranteed multi-question topic. Medical necessity, precertification vs prior authorization, and the ABN are all tested. Students who can explain these in their own words — not just define them — score significantly better.
🏥 CLINIC SHIFT — Coding Practice + Referral Simulation ~95 min
  • 1
    Activity 3 — Coding 101. ACT 3 — Individual, 10 minutes. Students categorize 6 scenarios by code type and write a rationale. Walk the room. The flu shot (HCPCS) and portable oxygen concentrator (HCPCS) scenarios trip students up most. Let them sit with the confusion before discussing — productive struggle is the goal here.
  • 2
    Activity 7 — Forms Knowledge Check. ACT 7 — No notes, 10 minutes. Six short answer questions: ABN, superbill, EOB vs RA, A/R aging report, precertification, and two-identifier rule. Collect or self-score. These 6 questions are specifically chosen because they reflect CCMA exam language.
  • 3
    EMR — Perform eligibility checks for all 3 patients. EMR MOD 9 — Walk students through the difference between verifying at scheduling vs. verifying day-of. Destiny's Medicaid must be verified today even though it was checked when scheduled. This is a common real-world failure point — make it memorable.
  • 4
    EMR — Facilitate Marcus's referral + prior auth. EMR MOD 8 — Walk students through the steps: identify specialist needed → check if prior auth is required → complete the pre-auth request in EMR → document. This is the most complex admin task of the week. Do a live demo first, then let students replicate.
  • 5
    ABN scenario — Robert Nguyen. Using the scenario card, have students explain to a "patient" (partner) what an ABN is and why they need to sign it. The script doesn't need to be perfect — the goal is that they can explain it without using jargon. This is a real-world skill that examiners and employers both notice.
  • 6
    Module 9 Quiz. MOD 9 — Open last 20 minutes or assign as homework. Note: Mod 9 is the longest quiz (9 skills). Students who have been working in the EMR all week tend to do well — connect that explicitly.
🌙 END OF SHIFT — Debrief ~25 min
  • 1
    Debrief the coding activity. Go through each of the 6 scenarios in Activity 3 as a class. Ask students to explain their reasoning, not just the answer. The flu shot HCPCS question always generates good discussion — lean into it.
  • 2
    Check EMR referral status. Every student should have Marcus's referral initiated. If anyone is missing this, have them complete it before they leave. This is a rubric check-off item.
  • 3
    Preview Day 4. "Tomorrow is our full billing simulation — checkout, claim submission, denied claims. We're running the entire back half of the revenue cycle. Make sure your Activities packet is up to date and your EMR tasks from today are done."
DAY 4 · THU
Billing + Full Clinic Simulation Run
Checkout · AVS · Claim Submission · Denial Management · Patient Responsibility · Mods 10, 12, 13
☀️ Huddle: 20 min 🏥 Shift: 100 min 🌙 Debrief: 25 min
☀️ MORNING HUDDLE ~20 min
  • 1
    Task list — where are we? SLIDE 6 — Pull up the Clinic Shift task list. Do a quick show of hands for each required task — who completed it, who hasn't. This isn't punitive — it's a real-time gap check so you know where to focus energy today.
  • 2
    Billing overview — 5 minutes, key points only. Hit three things: (1) claim must be clean before it goes out, (2) the clearinghouse is the middleman, (3) remittance advice tells you what happened. Everything else is in the activities.
  • 3
    Set the simulation frame. "Today you are running the back half of the revenue cycle for all three patients. Checkout, AVS, claim, denial response. You have everything you need in the EMR and your packet. Let's go."
🏥 CLINIC SHIFT — Full Billing Simulation ~100 min
  • 1
    EMR — Check out all 3 patients. EMR MOD 10 — Each checkout must include: status change, AVS generated, provider instructions documented. Marcus also needs his referral reflected in the checkout. Have students generate and "review" the AVS with their partner as if they're the patient.
  • 2
    EMR — Identify ICD-10-CM codes for each encounter. EMR MOD 12 — Students reference the superbill to select the correct diagnosis code. Marcus: T2DM uncontrolled + essential hypertension. Destiny: preventive exam, new patient. Robert: chest pain with dyspnea. Post codes in the EMR billing section.
  • 3
    EMR — Initiate claim submission. EMR MOD 13 — Walk through the billing section together as a class first, then let students navigate independently. They don't need to transmit a real claim — they need to demonstrate they can navigate the process and identify required fields.
  • 4
    Activity 6 — Denied Claim Decision Tree. ACT 6 — 12 minutes. Five denial scenarios, each requiring a specific next step and a rationale. This is high-yield CCMA content — denial management is a guaranteed exam topic. After individual work, review as a class with specific emphasis on the "lack of medical necessity" denial (most common and most tested).
  • 5
    Module Quizzes 10, 12, 13. MOD 10 MOD 12 MOD 13 — Assign whichever haven't been completed yet. These three are shorter than Mod 9 — students who have done the EMR work today should be able to move through them in 15–20 minutes total.
✅
RUBRIC CHECK — End of Day 4 Before students leave today, every item in the required EMR task list should be complete. Walk the room and verify: all 3 patients scheduled, checked in, checked out with AVS, at least one ICD-10-CM code posted, claim initiated. Students who are missing tasks should use any remaining time to catch up — tomorrow is badge day.
🌙 END OF SHIFT — Debrief ~25 min
  • 1
    Denial decision tree review. Go through the 5 denial scenarios together. For each one, ask students to first state their answer, then explain why. The "resubmit the claim" trap (answer for duplicate denial scenario) always catches students — make sure they understand why resubmitting a denied claim creates a duplicate denial.
  • 2
    Final check: all module quizzes. Mods 8, 9, 10, 12, and 13 should all be submitted. If any are incomplete, they become tonight's homework — badge day is tomorrow and quizzes must be done first.
  • 3
    Preview Friday. "Tomorrow you demonstrate what you know. Revenue cycle recitation is first — no notes. Then check-off. Then badge. Come in ready." Consider giving students the 6 debrief questions from Slide 8 to review tonight as prep.
DAY 5 · FRI
Check-Off Day + Admin Pro Badge
Revenue Cycle Recitation · Skill Demonstrations · Debrief Questions · Skills Passport Stamp
☀️ Huddle: 15 min 🏥 Check-Off: 110 min 🌙 Closing: 20 min
☀️ MORNING HUDDLE ~15 min
  • 1
    Debrief slide — orient to the day. SLIDE 8 — Review what today looks like: recitation first, then check-offs, then badge. Students who complete all check-off items earn the stamp. No pressure — just demonstration.
  • 2
    Last 5 minutes — silent review. Give students 5 minutes to silently review their notes, the revenue cycle order, and the debrief questions. No new content. Just space to prepare.

Run stations simultaneously while you rotate. Students who finish their check-off early work on peer review of each other's Activities packets or complete any remaining guided notes.

🌙 END OF SHIFT — Closing + Badge Ceremony ~20 min
  • 1
    Closing slide. SLIDE 9 — Read the tagline together: "You just ran an entire clinic visit from first phone call to claim submission." Let that land. Students often underestimate how much admin work they can now do.
  • 2
    Badge stamp ceremony. Call students individually or in groups to receive their Skills Passport stamp. Keep it brief but intentional — this is a Phase 3 milestone. For students who didn't quite earn the badge, privately let them know what they need to complete next week and that the path is still open.
  • 3
    Preview Week 15 — Coding Deep-Dive. SLIDE 9 — "Next week we go deeper on coding. You need your guided notes from this week — they're the foundation." Tell students to hold onto their Activities packet (or the graded version) as a study reference.
QUICK REFERENCE — COMMON STUDENT ERRORS BY DAY
DAY MOST COMMON ERROR HOW TO ADDRESS IT
Day 1 Putting Coding before Documentation in the revenue cycle; confusing EHR and EMR Use the "EMR is one visit, EHR is a whole life" anchor phrase. Revisit cycle order with a quick timeline visual on the board.
Day 2 Not verifying insurance before scheduling; skipping 2-identifier check at check-in Role-play a scenario where the insurance is lapsed — what happens to the clinic? Make the consequence concrete.
Day 3 Confusing ICD-10-CM and CPT; thinking HCPCS is only for hospitals Use the flu shot example — it's outpatient, it's an MA task, and it's HCPCS. That breaks the mental model quickly.
Day 4 Resubmitting a denied claim (creates duplicate); not knowing what to do after a "medical necessity" denial Write on the board: "Denied ≠ Resubmit. Denied = Appeal." Then walk through the appeal process step by step.
Day 5 Stalling on revenue cycle recitation; skipping "who is responsible" part Allow one "phone a friend" lifeline per student during recitation — it reduces anxiety without compromising the learning.
